My name is Adam Conway. I am the Lead Technical Editor for XDA-Developers. I have been a freelance technology journalist and reporter since 2017, producing content for websites such as The College Tribune and PixelSpot. I founded Irish Tech, a news site focused on the Irish technology market. I am also a Computer Science graduate from University College Dublin, having achieved a Second Class Honours, Grade 1, with an overall GPA of 3.54 in 2021. I achieved a First Class Honours in my final year and Bachelor's Thesis.

In my spare time, I like to investigate potential leads for a story, often putting my technical skills to use in order to try and find information that may not necessarily be public yet. I am also an avid competitor in the game Counter-Strike: 2, having been greatly involved as a volunteer in the Irish Collegiate Esports and the Irish Challenger League. I have written esports-centric articles for Team Dignitas, the Read TL;DR newsletter, and Rush B Media. I have also covered VALORANT events, such as the Valhalla Invitational as a reporter and editor.

I have been featured in multiple leading Irish news sites for purchasing the correctly spelled domain of an Irish-government-owned website, as the official website spelled the word "certificate" incorrectly. This was done to prevent it from being bought and used by scammers.

I have also been featured in multiple leading news organisations, including the UK Independent, for my reverse engineering of Xiaomi's alleged "blacklist" to uncover that it was a filtration service for advertisements, rather than a device-wide content censorship mechanism.
